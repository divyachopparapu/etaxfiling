    Deduction 80CCD

    Deduction in resepect contribution to a national pension system (NPS) [sec.80CCD]

    237A.These provisions are given below-

  34. Conditions-Section 80CCD is applicable if the following conditions are satisfied

      35. 1.The taxpayer is an indiduval.
      2.He is employee by the central Governament(on or after january 1,2004),or employed by any other person He may be even a self-employed person.
      3.He has in the previous year paid or deposited any amount in his account under NPS.NPS covers New pension Scheme(notified under Notification No.5/7/2003-ECB&PR,dated December 22,2003) and Atal pension yojana(as per Notification No.SO 529(E),dated February 19,2016).
  35. Consequences if the above conditions are satisfied -if the above conditions are satisfied, then the following Consequences given by section 80CCD should be noted-

      36. 1.Conditions toward NPS by employee (or any other indiduval assessee) [Sec. 80CCD(1)] Asssess's contribution to NPS is deductible under section 80CCD(1)in the year in which contribu-tion is made. No deduction is available in resepect of employee's contribution which is in excess of 10 per cent of the salary of the employee .Likewise, if contrbution by a taxpayer (not being an employee)Exceed 10 per cent (20 per cent from the assenment year 2018-19) of his gross total income, the excess shall not be taken into consideration for the purpose of section 80CCD (1).
      2.Contribution toward NPS by employer[Sec 80CCD(2) contribution by the employer to NPS is deductible under section 80CCD(2) in the hands of the concerned employee in the year which Contribution is made. However,no deduction is available in resepect of employer's contribution which is in excess of 10 per cent [or(with effects from the asseessment year 2020-21) 14 per cent, if employer is the central Governament]of the salary of the employee.
      3.Cumulative monetary celling -By virtue of secection 80CCE, the aggreate amount of deduction under section 80C,80CCC and 80CCD(1)[i.e contribution by an employee(or any other indiduval) toward NPS] cannot exceed Rs.1,50,000 The ceiling limit is not applicable (from the assessment year 2012-13) in resepect of employer's contribution toward NPS. Moreover, the ceiling limit will not be applicable in respect of an assessee's contribution toward NPS under section 80CCD(1B)from the assessment year 2016-17 which is given below.
      4.Additional contribution up to Rs.50,000 towards NPS under section 80CCd(1B)-sub-section (1B) has been intersted in section 80CCD(with effect from the asseessment year 2016-17)so as to provide for an additional deduction in resepet of any amount paid (up to Rs.50,000)for contributions made by any individual assessee under the NPS. On this contribution, the ceiling of Rs.1,50,000 under section 80CCE will not be applicable. The additional deduction of Rs.50,000 is available whether (or not) any claim under section 80CCD(1) has been made-Circular No.19/2015, dated November27,2015. For instance, X (who is covered by NPS) gets salary of Rs.8,00,000 per annum. He annually contributes 10percent of salary towards NPS and Rs.1,21,000 towards PPF/LIP since 2014. For the assessment year 2020-21, he can claim deduction as follows-
  36. RS.50,00 under section
  37. RS.30,000 under section 80CCD(1)
  38. Rs.1,20,000 under section 80C

    39. X has not made any additional Investment over and above the minimum requirement of 10 per cent contribution toward NPS.But he can claim deduction of RS.50,000 under section 80CCD(1B).
